Hi, I am Jiashu Yu.

I am an undergraduate student at Tsinghua University (Xingjian College), pursuing a dual degree in Mechanics and Interdisciplinary Engineering.

My research lies at the intersection of **robotics, reinforcement learning, and embodied AI**, with a particular focus on building intelligent agents that can reason, perceive, and act in complex real-world environments.

I am currently working with Prof. Jiangtao Gong at the Institute for AI Industry Research, where I study **LLM/VLM-based autonomous driving systems**.

Previously, I worked on designing a hierarchical evaluation framework for autonomous driving using large language models, combining multi-modal perception, simulation (CARLA), and human evaluation to assess driving behavior across multiple levels of decision-making.

I have also gained industry research experience at Dongfeng Motor Corporation (AI Lab), where I developed an automated VQA annotation pipeline for real-world vehicle data, leveraging vision-language models and structured reasoning to generate high-quality multi-modal datasets.

In Summer 2026, I will join the NYU AI4CE Lab as a visiting research student under Prof. Chen Feng, where I will further explore learning-based approaches for embodied intelligence.

More broadly, I am interested in:
- Embodied AI and robotic learning  
- Reinforcement learning for decision-making  
- Vision-language-action models  
- Human-level intelligence in autonomous systems
